Black holes

Black holes are incredibly dense regions in space where gravity is so strong that not even light can escape. They form when massive stars collapse at the end of their life cycle, creating a point of infinite density called a singularity, surrounded by an invisible boundary known as the event horizon.

There are different types of black holes, ranging from stellar black holes a few times the mass of the Sun to supermassive ones found at the centers of galaxies. While they donâ€™t pull everything in, they strongly influence nearby stars, gas, and light through their immense gravity.

Scientists study black holes by observing their effects on surrounding matter and by detecting signals like gravitational waves. Discoveries such as the first image of a black hole have opened new doors to understanding space, time, and the limits of physics.
